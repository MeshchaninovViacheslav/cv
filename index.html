01 — Attention Mechanisms as a Foundation of Modern Neural Architectures

This article examines the role of attention mechanisms as a core architectural primitive in contemporary neural networks. We trace the conceptual shift from recurrent processing to attention-based computation and discuss its implications for scalability, parallelism, and representational capacity. The analysis suggests that attention is best understood not as a single technique, but as a general principle for routing information across structured inputs.
